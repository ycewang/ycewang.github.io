About Me

I am a senior at UC Davis, double majoring in Chemistry and Materials Science and Engineering. I was most recently a materials engineering intern at HP, working at the Vancouver Science Labs in Vancouver, WA. My internship projects at HP focused on characterizing sustainable materials for product packaging, as well as processes for qualifying used printer parts for sustainable reuse.

Before HP, I was a materials engineering intern in the Analytical Lab at S&C Electric in Chicago, IL. My responsibilities centered around materials characterization for quality assurance and process improvements. For my internship project, I developed test methods for analyzing braze penetration in copper-chromium powdered metal parts. I then conducted experiments that test levels of braze penetration for different processes. The results of my experiment determined the final fabrication process for production.

Currently, I am an undergraduate researcher in Prof. Susan Kauzlarich's research group at UC Davis, researching thermoelectric materials. I am focused on the synthesis of antimony telluride nanoplates (shown below!).
